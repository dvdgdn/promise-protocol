PROMISE PROTOCOL

Infrastructure for Verifiable Trust Through Domain-Specific Promises

1. The Problem & Market Opportunity

Core Problem: The information tsunami has rendered credibility evaluation impossible online. Anyone can make claims; verifying them takes time, expertise, and risk. This creates adverse selection where dishonesty is rewarded.

Total Addressable Market (TAM)

  • Trust/Verification Services: $45B+ global market
  • Initial Wedge (Germany): ~27K Tragfähigkeitsbescheinigungen/year
  • Adjacent Markets: Professional services verification, AI capability assessment, B2B growth claims
  • Expansion: Any domain with measurable outcomes and consequential claims

Why Now?

  • AI Flood: LLMs make generating plausible claims trivial
  • Verification Crisis: Traditional reputation systems failing at scale
  • Economic Pressure: Markets demand higher-fidelity trust signals
  • Regulatory Tailwind: EU AI Act creates demand for capability verification

7. Core Trust Mechanisms

Economic Barriers (Primary Defense)

  • Progressive staking costs: Creating fake identities becomes exponentially expensive
  • Slashing mechanism: Dishonest promises/assessments lose staked credits
  • Reward pool: Slashed stakes fund rewards for honest actors
  • Result: Honesty becomes the utility-maximizing strategy

Merit-Weighted Assessment

  • Domain-specific accumulation: Can't transfer success from one area to another
  • Track record required: New agents face stricter evidence requirements
  • Assessor influence scales with merit: Proven experts carry more weight
  • Result: Manipulation requires building real credibility first
